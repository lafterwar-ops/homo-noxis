\typeprologue

Clémentine,\par

I cannot ask for forgiveness without first setting down the chain of events that made history unfold as it did. The difficulty is knowing where such a chain begins. \par

The beginning, what a strange word. I could begin with myself: a young scientist studying seismic resonance and chain reactions. I could begin with the men who abducted me, and later broke me in rooms no court will ever inspect. I could begin with the oasis and the mountain, found exactly as they had been described to me.\par

In the beginning God created the heaven and the earth. And the earth was without form, and void; and darkness was upon the face of the deep. And the Spirit of God moved upon the face of the waters. And God said, Let there be light: and there was light.\par

That is at least one chapter too far back, and, somehow, not far enough. In 1998, while working for an organization unapologetically called "The Outsiders", I met a young man in Switzerland who said his first name had once been Kailan.\par

This is his account, and the first link in mine. \par

Paul Morgan \par
