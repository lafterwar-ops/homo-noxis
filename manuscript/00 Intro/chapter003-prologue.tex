\typeprologue

Clémentine,\par

I cannot ask for your forgiveness without first setting down the chain of events that made history unfold as it did. The difficulty is knowing where such a chain begins. \par

The beginning, what a strange word. I could begin with myself: a young scientist studying seismic resonance and chain reactions. I could begin with the men who abducted me, and later broke me in rooms no court will ever inspect. I could begin with the oasis and the mountain, found exactly as they had been described to me.\par

\begin{scripture}
In the beginning God created the heaven and the earth. 
\end{scripture}

Starting there would be at least one chapter too far back, and, somehow, not far enough. \par

In 1998, while working for an organization unapologetically called "The Outsiders", I met a young man in Switzerland who said his first name had once been Kailan.\par

This my retranscription of his account, and the first link in my story. \par

Paul Morgan. \par
